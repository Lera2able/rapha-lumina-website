# Google Ads starter campaign

This is a practical starter build for `web.raphalumina.com`, focused on high-intent Google Search traffic in South Africa.

Use this as the first campaign structure before expanding into display, remarketing, or Meta ads.

## Objective

Generate qualified leads for:

- business websites
- ecommerce websites
- school websites
- website redesigns
- private portals and business tools

## Recommended campaign type

- Campaign type: `Search`
- Goal: `Leads`
- Networks: `Google Search` only
- Turn off: `Display Network`

## Location targeting

Start with:

- `South Africa`

If budget is tight, narrow first to your strongest service areas.

## Language

- `English`

## Bidding

Start with one of these:

- If conversion tracking is already working: `Maximize Conversions`
- If conversion tracking is not confirmed yet: `Maximize Clicks` with a CPC cap

## Daily budget

Suggested starting range:

- Total: `R100 to R200 per day`

Suggested split:

- `Web Design SA`: 40%
- `Small Business Websites`: 25%
- `Ecommerce Websites`: 20%
- `School Websites`: 15%

## Conversion actions to track

Before scaling spend, confirm these are tracked:

- quote form submission
- contact form submission
- WhatsApp click
- phone click

## Ad strength tips

To improve ad strength:

- use at least `10 to 15` headlines
- use at least `4` descriptions
- include the main keyword in some headlines
- include pricing in relevant ads
- include one trust/proof line like `See Real Client Work`

## Launch checklist

Before launch:

- confirm conversions are tracking
- confirm phone and WhatsApp clicks are counted
- confirm all landing pages load fast on mobile
- confirm forms are working
- confirm business number and email are correct
- add negative keywords at campaign level
- turn off Display Network

## Week 1 review checklist

After 5 to 7 days, review:

- search terms report
- CTR by ad group
- cost per click
- conversion count
- WhatsApp clicks
- wasted spend on irrelevant searches

Pause keywords or add negatives if you see traffic from:

- students
- job seekers
- people looking for free website builders
- people searching for courses or definitions

## Recommended next move after launch
